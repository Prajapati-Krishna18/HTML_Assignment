Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made of crispy rice crepes filled with a spiced potato filling. It is usually served with coconut chutney and sambar.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dosa batter
    • Homemade batter (recommended)
    • Store-bought batter (alternative)
  • 2 boiled potatoes
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt (to taste)

Cooking Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter.
  2. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add green chili, boiled potatoes, and salt. Mix well and set aside.
  4. Pour dosa batter onto a hot tawa and spread thin.
  5. Place potato mixture inside, fold, and serve hot.

Cooking Terms

Splutter
The popping sound spices make when added to hot oil.
Sauté
To cook quickly in a small amount of oil.

Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a rich and creamy North Indian curry made with paneer cubes simmered in a tomato-based gravy with butter and cream.

Ingredients

  • 200g paneer (cottage cheese)
    • Fresh paneer (recommended)
    • Frozen paneer (alternative)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ cup fresh cream
  • Salt (to taste)

Cooking Steps

  1. Melt butter in a pan and sauté onions until soft.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  3. Add tomato puree, salt, and garam masala. Simmer until oil separates.
  4. Add paneer cubes and cream, mix well, and cook for a few minutes.
  5. Serve hot with naan or rice.

Cooking Terms

Simmer
To cook food gently in liquid just below boiling point.
Gravy
A thickened sauce made from cooked vegetables, spices, and liquid.

Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a famous Indian sweet made of milk solids that are fried into soft balls and soaked in fragrant sugar syrup flavored with cardamom and rose water.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup khoya (milk solids)
    • Fresh homemade khoya (best flavor)
    • Store-bought khoya (quick alternative)
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • Oil or ghee (for frying)
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tsp cardamom powder
  • ½ tsp rose water

Cooking Steps

  1. Mix khoya, flour, and baking soda into a soft dough. Shape into small balls.
  2. Heat oil/ghee on low flame and fry balls until golden brown.
  3. Prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, cardamom, and rose water.
  4. Soak fried balls in warm syrup for at least 1 hour.
  5. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Cooking Terms

Dough
A soft mixture of flour and liquid used for baking or frying.
Syrup
A thick liquid made by dissolving sugar in water, often flavored.
